Insecticides

Consequences

   There are many different classes of insecticides, and quite a lot of them have adverse effects to other animals, including humans. This is generally suboptimal. As an example, DDT has been shown to reduce the thickness of bird eggshells, making them more prone to cracking, and reducing bird populations.

   On top of that, there is the consideration that this would eradicate mosquitoes as a clade, meaning that there will be other ripple effects to the ecosystem.

   Even more concerningly, many pests have a tendency to develop resistances to pesticides, meaning that this is not a one-time solution, and more pesticides must be discovered to maintain a low mosquito population, potentially with even more side effects, and at a higher cost with each iteration.
