Insecticides

Consequences

   There are many classes of insecticides, and many have adverse effects on other animals, including humans. This is generally undesirable. For example, DDT has been shown to thin bird eggshells, making them more prone to cracking and leading to population declines.

   Additionally, eradicating mosquitoes as a group would have ripple effects throughout the ecosystem.

   More concerningly, many pests develop resistance to pesticides, meaning this is not a one-time solution. New pesticides must continually be developed to keep mosquito populations low, potentially leading to more side effects and increasing costs over time.
