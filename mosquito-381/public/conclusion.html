Conclusion

    After analysis of the provided solutions, each does have their own potential concerns, some more than others, but we have decided that mosquito nets have the most advantageous combination of features, between the lack of severe collateral damage, having a high probability of success within the defined range, and relatively affordable cost.

Nukes

   The issue with the nukes should be fairly obvious. While it is relatively cheap and quite likely to be effective, the side effects of also eradicating all life has proven to be too significant to overlook. It did, however, offer a lovely benchmark to compare the other methods against, so it should at least be given credit for that.

Dunks

   The consequences are less severe, but that has been traded in for costing three orders of magnitude more, which is a bit expensive compared to the mosquito nets.

Insecticides

   Insecticides are generally ineffective in the long-term and tend to cause harm to other animals. This being chosen above mosquito nets is already unlikely, but the treatment is unable to produce the same probability of complete eradication as the others, all while costing quite a lot.

Genetic Modification

   Genetically modified mosquitoes suffer from many of the same issues as Insecticides, as both have severe consequences, an inability to make the benchmark, and costing quite a lot.
