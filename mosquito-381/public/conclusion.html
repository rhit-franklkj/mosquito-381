Conclusion

    After analyzing the proposed solutions, each has its own concernsâ€”some more significant than others. However, mosquito nets offer the most advantageous combination of features: minimal collateral damage, a high probability of success within the defined range, and a relatively affordable cost.

Nukes

   The issue with the nukes should be fairly obvious. While it is relatively cheap and quite likely to be effective, the side effects of also eradicating all life has proven to be too significant to overlook. It did, however, offer a lovely benchmark to compare the other methods against, so it should at least be given credit for that.

Dunks

   While the consequences are less severe, this method is three orders of magnitude more expensive than mosquito nets, making it a costly alternative.

Insecticides

   Insecticides are generally ineffective in the long term and often harm other animals. Choosing them over mosquito nets is unlikely, as they fail to achieve the same probability of complete eradication while remaining costly.

Genetic Modification

   Genetically modified mosquitoes share many of the same issues as insecticides: severe consequences, failure to meet the benchmark, and high costs.
