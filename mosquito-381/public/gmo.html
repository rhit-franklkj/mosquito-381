Genetic Modification

Consequences

   The example of genetically modified mosquitoes released in Florida specifically involved the species Aedes aegypti. Among mosquitoes targeted for modification, this is one of the two reasonable choices.

   The other is the genus Anopheles, which, as discussed earlier, is the genus that spreads malaria. The majority of mosquito deaths are a result of malaria, meaning that while modifying A. aegypti will improve the situation, the main issue is still very apparent.

   Theoretically, if the same technology can be applied to Anopheles mosquitoes, this could be a viable solution

   The ecological risks of eradicating all mosquitoes are reduced but not eliminated. Eliminating entire genera could still have ecological consequences, even if the genetic modifications themselves do not cause issues.
