Nukes

Consequences

   Everything, including humans, would die—except perhaps a handful of tardigrades. While not the ideal solution, it would strip mosquitoes of their title as the deadliest animal to humans, since humanity itself would be wiped out in a short period. The explosion, radiation, and ensuing nuclear winter would also drive many staple crops to extinction. It would be quite a lot like the end-Permian extinction (not the one that resulted in the extinction of non-avian dinosaurs. It's the one right before the dinosaurs), except it happens in a year instead of several million.

Probability

   There is a 99% chance the mosquitoes would be eradicated. This would be promising if the consequences were not so severe.

Cost

   $2 billion may seem like a lot, but in the aftermath, capitalism—and the economy itself—would cease to exist, making the cost almost irrelevant. However, it serves as a useful benchmark for estimating the cost of other eradication methods.
